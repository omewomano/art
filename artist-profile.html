Julie Mehretu

Julie Mehretu is an American artist known for her large scale achitectural abstractions. Working as a painter and printmaker, Mehretu layers pigment withb graphite ink into heavily intersected lines of color, topographical element, and geometrical and free-form renderings. Born on November 28 1970 in Addis Ababa, Ethiopia, her family moved to the United States in 1977. she graduated from Kalamazoo college in 1992, and begun pursuing an art carrier independently before she enrolled in the MFA program at the Rhode Island school of design. She blends element of abstract expressionism with Pop Art, and her work bears the influence of 20th century abstractionist such as Wassilly, Kandisky, Kazimir Malevich and Piet Mondrian. Mehretu currently leaves and works in New York, NY. She was the recipient of the MacArthur fellowship in 2005.
